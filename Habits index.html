Habits


If an individual becomes symptomatic, where inpatient facilities do not exist for all COVID-19 patients, for mild to moderate cases of COVID-19, such individuals should stay at home and be “isolated”, i.e. separate themselves from others in the household. You should look at the information below :
  • Caregivers and anyone who has been in close contact with someone who has COVID-19 should stay home.
  • The patient must be isolated in a room alone.
  • He should use disposable tools in eating or in bathroom or anything.
  • He shouldn’t get out of the house for any reason.
  • He should clean his hands regularly and constantly sterilizing his room.
  • He should limit contact with people either with his family as possible.
  • He should follow the doctor’s instructions and take his medicine regularly.
  • Patient should talk to the doctor if he feels worse.
  • Caregivers and the patient should use masks and gloves when communicating with other people.
  • Cleaning and disinfecting rooms and all parts of the house constantly.
  • He cannot transport to the hospital or any place without his doctor’s permission and he should use an ambulance.
